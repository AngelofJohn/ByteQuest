# FINANCIAL PLAN

## Revenue Model

### Recommended Pricing Structure

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Free Demo** | $0 | Dawnmere zone, limited vocabulary |
| **Full Game** | $15-25 | All zones, full story, all content |
| **Supporter Edition** | $30-40 | Full game + soundtrack, art book PDF |

### Model Options

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| **One-Time Purchase** | Simple, player-friendly | Limited revenue per user |
| **Demo + Full Game** | Try before buy, builds trust | Must nail the demo |
| **Expansion Packs** | Ongoing revenue | Must deliver value each time |

### Future Revenue Streams

| Stream | When |
|--------|------|
| DLC / Expansions (new languages) | Post-launch |
| Soundtrack | With launch |
| Art Book PDF | With launch |

# MARKETING STRATEGY

## Target Audience

### Primary Audiences

| Audience | Where They Are |
|----------|----------------|
| Language learners (frustrated with Duolingo) | Reddit, YouTube, language forums |
| RPG enthusiasts | Steam, Discord, Twitter/X |
| Indie game fans | itch.io, Twitter/X, YouTube |
| Educators | Education forums, LinkedIn |

### Audience Personas

**"The Frustrated Learner"** (25-40)
- Tried Duolingo, lost interest
- Wants depth over gamification gimmicks
- Found on: Reddit, YouTube, podcasts

**"The RPG Fan"** (18-35)
- Loves story-driven games
- Appreciates pixel art
- Found on: Steam, Discord, Twitter/X

---

## Brand Identity

### Core Message

> "Learn French through adventure, not algorithms."

### Value Propositions

| For Language Learners | For Gamers |
|-----------------------|------------|
| Actually engaging way to learn | Unique RPG with real progression |
| Story gives context to vocabulary | Meaningful choices |
| No streak anxiety or manipulation | Pixel art, rich world |

### Positioning Statement

> "For language learners who want engagement without manipulation, ByteQuest is an RPG that teaches French through adventure. Unlike Duolingo's streak-anxiety approach, we offer meaningful story progression where your vocabulary is your weapon."
