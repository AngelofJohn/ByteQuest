## 4. Data Subject Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights. We respond to all requests within 30 days.

### 4.1 Right of Access (Article 15)

You may request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Data Access Request"

**We will provide:**
- Categories of data processed
- Purposes of processing
- Recipients of data
- Retention periods
- Source of data (if not collected from you)
- Existence of automated decision-making

### 4.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You may request correction of inaccurate personal data.

**How to exercise:**
- Update directly in account settings, OR
- Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Rectification Request"

### 4.3 Right to Erasure / "Right to be Forgotten" (Article 17)

You may request deletion of your personal data when:

- Data is no longer necessary for original purpose
- You withdraw consent (where consent was the basis)
- You object to processing and no overriding legitimate grounds exist
- Data was unlawfully processed
- Legal obligation requires erasure

**Exceptions:** We may retain data required for:
- Legal compliance
- Exercise or defense of legal claims
- Public interest archiving

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Erasure Request"

### 4.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Article 18)

You may request we limit processing when:

- You contest data accuracy (during verification)
- Processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction over erasure
- We no longer need the data but you need it for legal claims
- You have objected to processing (pending verification)

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Restriction Request"

### 4.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You may receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

**Applies to:**
- Data you provided to us
- Data processed by automated means
- Data processed based on consent or contract

**Format provided:** JSON or CSV (your choice)

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Portability Request"

### 4.6 Right to Object (Article 21)

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests.

**How to exercise:** Email [DPO EMAIL] with subject "GDPR Objection"

**Direct marketing:** You have an absolute right to object to direct marketing at any time.

### 4.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

**Current status:** ByteQuest does not use automated decision-making that produces legal effects. Game mechanics (e.g., difficulty adjustment) are not considered "significant effects" under GDPR.

## 6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary:

| Data Category | Retention Period | Basis |
|---------------|------------------|-------|
| Account data | Until account deletion + 30 days | Contractual |
| Game progress | Until account deletion | Contractual |
| Payment records | 7 years | Legal obligation |
| Support tickets | 2 years after resolution | Legitimate interest |
| Analytics (aggregated) | Indefinite | Legitimate interest |
| Error logs | 90 days | Legitimate interest |
| Marketing consent | Until withdrawn | Consent |

### 6.1 After Account Deletion

Upon account deletion request:

1. **Immediate:** Account deactivated, data anonymized in active systems
2. **30 days:** Personal data purged from primary databases
3. **90 days:** Personal data purged from backups
4. **Retained:** Anonymized analytics, legal-required records

## 8. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures:

### 8.1 Technical Measures

- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Password hashing (bcrypt)
- Regular security updates
- Firewall protection
- Intrusion detection

### 8.2 Organizational Measures

- Access controls (principle of least privilege)
- Staff training on data protection
- Incident response procedures
- Regular security audits
- Vendor due diligence

### 8.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach:

1. **Within 72 hours:** Notify supervisory authority (if risk to rights/freedoms)
2. **Without undue delay:** Notify affected users (if high risk)
3. **Document:** Record all breaches regardless of notification requirement
